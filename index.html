Why is Forensic Science important?

Forensic science is crucial to possess and maintain an effective justice system, along with a civil society. Without the use of Forensic Science, our society would remain unsafe, unsecure, and dangerous. Investigations of domestic and international incidents would continue to be unsolved due to the lack of researched evidence. Furthermore, Forensic science also plays a critically important role in U.S. national security, as well as ensuring public health and safety. Before Forensics Science was majorly recognized, police departments and jails would simply only take a copy of a criminals fingerprints for safekeeping. However now that Forensics has been more used in modern day investigations, police collect more aspects of DNA such as hair strands, blood samples, along with that fingerprint copy, which are all used to uncover all types of crimes and mysteries preventing another chance of them repeating again and keeping everyone safe.

Forensic Scientists:

Forensic professionals working in law enforcement, government, or private forensic laboratories who are responsible for dealing with any number of specific tests and analyses, such as toxicology, ballistics, trace evidence, etc.

Forensic Pathologists:

Can be reffered to as medical examiners that oversee and investigate in autopsies and clinical forensic examinations.

Associated Scientists

Scientific professionals lending their knowledge to forensic science, such as forensic odontologists, forensic botanists, forensic anthropologists, etc. These scientists apply their knowledge to the forensic science field as to provide investigators with crucial information regarding everything from bite marks to insect infestation on the postmortem body..
